# Lower back injury exercise guidance

People with a history of disc herniation or acute lower back pain are generally advised to avoid loaded spinal flexion under load, such as heavy deadlifts, good mornings, or sit-ups performed with a rounded lower back.

Conventional deadlifts and barbell back squats place significant axial load on the lumbar spine; for people with lower back issues, trap-bar deadlifts and goblet squats are often recommended as lower-risk substitutes since they allow a more upright torso.

Core stability work such as planks, bird-dogs, and dead bugs is commonly recommended for people rehabbing lower back injuries because it builds trunk stability without spinal flexion or heavy axial loading.

Twisting loaded movements (e.g., loaded Russian twists, barbell rotations) are generally discouraged for people with disc-related back pain due to combined flexion-rotation stress on the spine.

Walking, swimming, and light stationary cycling are commonly recommended as safe low-impact cardio options during lower back injury recovery.
